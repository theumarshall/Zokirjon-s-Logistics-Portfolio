About this portfolio

Logistics is the part of a supply chain that moves and stores materials — from raw resources, through factories and warehouses, to the customer, and sometimes back again. This course traced that journey end to end: how goods flow, what each move costs, which transportation mode fits which shipment, where products are stored, and who you hire to manage it all.

Rather than collect screenshots of completed tasks, this site turns five of those concepts into things you can actually use. Two are reading-based explainers; three are working models I built — a freight cost calculator, a mode-selection engine, and a warehouse strategy comparator. Every project follows the same manifest: an overview of why it matters, a hands-on demonstration, evidence drawn from the coursework, and what I took away from it.

Guiding principle: show the concept I learned, not simply the assignment I completed. A visitor should understand each idea within a few minutes.
