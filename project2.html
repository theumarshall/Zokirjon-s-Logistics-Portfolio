The Cost of Logistics

In a low-margin business, one missed variable turns a profitable lane into a loss. This calculator prices an over-the-road freight move the way a carrier actually has to.

1

Project overview

Logistics cost is simply the total cost of moving freight from one place to another — but "simply" hides a lot. Chapter 3 breaks an over-the-road (OTR) trucking move into fixed costs (paid whether or not the truck hauls anything) and variable costs (which dominate — roughly 70% of OTR cost). Get the mix wrong and the move loses money.

  • What inspired it: the chapter's fixed/variable cost table, the fuel-surcharge formula, and the "elements of a move" — deadhead, pickup, bill of lading, highway miles, delivery, backhaul.
  • The concept: price a move by capturing every segment — including empty deadhead miles and idle waiting time — then recover fuel through a surcharge and offset cost with a paid backhaul.
  • Why it matters: "if the wheels ain't turnin', no one's earnin'." Idle time and empty miles are pure cost, and the calculator makes that visible instantly.

Fuel surcharge logic (modelled): surcharge per mile = (diesel price − peg rate) ÷ fuel economy, and never below zero. The carrier pays full diesel as a cost but recovers the portion above the peg from the shipper — exactly how the surcharge protects a carrier from rising fuel prices.
4

What did I learn?

Knowledge // concepts

  • Fixed vs variable cost, and why OTR is ~70% variable.
  • How a fuel surcharge works off a peg rate to offset diesel swings.
  • Why deadhead miles and idle time quietly destroy a lane's margin.

Skills // abilities

  • Cost modelling — turning a pricing concept into a live spreadsheet-style engine.
  • Data visualisation — composition and split charts that update in real time.
  • Break-even thinking — finding the price where a move stops losing money.

Reflection // real world

  • Surprising: how often a "good" lane is actually a small loss once deadhead is counted.
  • Difficult: separating fuel cost from fuel surcharge without double-counting.
  • Applied: this is the math behind every quote a freight broker gives a shipper.
